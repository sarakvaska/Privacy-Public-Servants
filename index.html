Overview


Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is defined as "any data that could potentially be used to identify a particular person."1 Examples of PII include a person's full name, date of birth, house address, and contact information, among others.

Much of this information for any individual can be found on the Internet, through online directory services, data collection agencies, federal websites, and social media.

When it comes to privacy, many people are wary of their PII being freely available online. For some, however, this information being publicly available can be dangerous.

In this project, we're going to examine how public servants (including politicians, judges, prosecutors, and law enforcement officers) should have the right to shield their PII from online directories and internet searches—as if they're forgotten from the internet.
